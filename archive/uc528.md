# Posture — App Store Release Readiness Audit

**Date:** 2026-05-28
**Build:** MARKETING_VERSION 1.0, CURRENT_PROJECT_VERSION 36
**Scope:** User-perspective audit for (1) getting through App Review, (2) not misleading users, (3) converting new installs into trial starts → paid.
**Method:** 6 parallel dimension audits over code + metadata, with adversarial re-verification of every blocker/high finding against the source. Findings marked ✅ were independently re-read and confirmed; ⚠️ were not double-checked by a second agent (lower confidence).

---

## TL;DR verdict

**Not submittable as-is.** Three hard blockers will get it rejected or block upload outright, and the conversion funnel is structurally capped even after approval. The good news: the binary itself is clean (no leftover camera code, paywall is well-built, failure handling is mostly graceful). The damage is concentrated in **(a) a calibration gate that bricks the app without AirPods** and **(b) stale "camera" metadata across every storefront**. Both are fixable without deep surgery.

The single most important fact: **an App Review tester almost certainly has no AirPods Pro, and right now they cannot get past the calibration screen into the app at all.** That alone is a guaranteed rejection.

## 🔴 BLOCKERS

### B1 — Calibration is a hard dead-end without AirPods (Guideline 2.1) ✅
*Reported independently by 4 of 6 audit dimensions; verified by re-reading the source.*

**What happens:** `RootView` (`App.swift:174-180`) routes Onboarding → **CalibrationView** → MainTabView, gated on `settings.hasCalibrated`. `hasCalibrated = true` is set in **exactly one place** — `CalibrationView.swift:204`, inside the `.done` step, which is only reachable after a successful AirPods motion capture (`guard pitch.count >= 5`, `CalibrationView.swift:279`). With no compatible AirPods:
- `begin()` waits `connectDeadlineSeconds = 30` (`CalibrationView.swift:41`), then flips to `.unsupported` (`:231`).
- The `.unsupported` step in onboarding mode (`:136-174`) shows **only a "Try Again" button** (`:161`). The Cancel button is gated behind `if mode == .quickRecalibrate` (`:165`), so onboarding has **no exit**. "Try Again" just re-arms the same 30s wait — an infinite loop.
- `HeadphoneMotionService.start()` confirms the trap: on a device/simulator without head-tracking AirPods, `isDeviceMotionAvailable` is false, `isConnected` never flips, no samples ever arrive.

**Also dead-ends on the iOS Simulator** (no `CMHeadphoneMotionManager` source) — and CLAUDE.md documents the Simulator as the primary build/run path. So there is **no environment** in which a reviewer lacking physical AirPods Pro can evaluate the app. There is no `targetEnvironment(simulator)` bypass and no review-mode flag (`UITEST_FRESH` only *resets* state, it doesn't seed a calibration).

**User/reviewer impact:** Tester taps Get Started → 30s on "Pop in your AirPods" → "We need compatible AirPods" with one button that loops → **never reaches Today, History, Settings, or the paywall.** Textbook 2.1 rejection. Any impulse-download user without AirPods on hand is equally bricked → instant churn → zero conversion.

**Fix:**
1. Add a low-emphasis escape on the onboarding `.waiting` and `.unsupported` steps — e.g. a ghost **"Continue without AirPods" / "Set up later"** that seeds a neutral baseline (the scan already falls back to `basePitch: 0` at `AirpodsScanView.swift:219`) and sets `hasCalibrated = true` (or a separate `calibrationDeferred` flag), dropping the user into MainTabView with a persistent "finish calibration" banner.
2. The manual check-in loop already works without AirPods (`AcknowledgmentView.swift:85-94` "just checking in, manual") — the fix is just to *let the user reach it*.
3. Pair with a **self-report quality** path (see M2) so the app is genuinely demonstrable without hardware.

### B2 — App Store listing still sells camera scans the binary no longer has (Guideline 2.3.1) ✅
*Verified verbatim across source files and locale dirs.*

The app pivoted to AirPods-only (camera capture removed: no `NSCameraUsageDescription` in `Info.plist`, no `AVCaptureSession`/`VNDetectFaceRectangles`/`import Vision` anywhere). But the public listing still advertises a camera feature:

- `fastlane/metadata/en-US/description.txt`:
  - `:12` THE FREE TIER → "Quick camera scans using on-device Vision (front camera)"
  - `:23` POSTURE+ → "Camera-free scans for AirPods users" *(nonsensical — the whole app is camera-free now; implies the free tier is camera-based)*
  - `:29` "Camera frames are processed on-device and immediately discarded"
  - `:33-34` an entire **"A NOTE ON THE CAMERA"** section
- `promotional_text.txt:1` → "do a 3-second camera check"
- `keywords.txt:1` → ends in `…,wfh,camera`

**Worse, it ships in every storefront:** the camera claim is present in **50/50** localized `description.txt` files and **32/50** `keywords.txt` files (e.g. de-DE "Schnelle Kamera-Scans … (Frontkamera)" + "HINWEIS ZUR KAMERA"; fr-FR "Scans caméra rapides"; es-ES "cámara frontal"; ja "前面カメラ"; localized keywords kamera/caméra/cámara/カメラ/카메라/相机).

**Compounding flag:** your own `review_information/notes.txt` correctly says "AirPods-only, no camera" — so the reviewer reads notes that **directly contradict** the live listing. Reviewers reject on notes-vs-metadata mismatch even when the binary is fine.

**Fix:** Run `scripts/pull-appstore-metadata.sh` first (snapshot), then rewrite description/promo/keywords to AirPods head-motion + manual fallback. Delete the "A NOTE ON THE CAMERA" block, the line-12 camera free-tier bullet, the line-23 "Camera-free scans" perk, and the line-29 camera privacy bullet. Remove `camera` from keywords. Re-translate the corrected en-US into every locale and grep each for the local word for camera/Vision to confirm zero matches before upload.

### B3 — No screenshots staged, and the upload pipeline is destructive ✅
*Verified.*

`fastlane/screenshots/en-US/` contains **0 files** (`find fastlane/screenshots -type f` → empty). App Store Connect will not let you submit a version without at least one 6.7"/6.9" iPhone screenshot.

**Materially worse than just "missing":** `fastlane/Fastfile:23-24` sets `skip_screenshots: false` + `overwrite_screenshots: true`, and `scripts/upload-appstore-metadata.sh` runs the `upload_metadata` lane. With an empty local dir, running the normal upload would **wipe any screenshots currently live on ASC** and replace them with nothing.

**Fix:** Capture current AirPods-flow screenshots — Today check-in, AirPods scan, streak/history, paywall — for the required sizes. **None may show a camera viewfinder** (that would re-trigger B2). Place under `fastlane/screenshots/en-US/` (+ locales as needed), then upload.

## 🟠 HIGH

### H1 — Paywall Restore / Terms / Privacy / disclosure only render when products load (Guideline 3.1.2) ✅
`PaywallView.swift:32-43`: the `content` + `stickyCTA` (which contain the price/trial/auto-renew **disclosure** `:336-351`, **Restore Purchases** `:258`, and **Terms/Privacy links** `:267-269`) only render inside `#if HAS_REVENUECAT` **and** when `subscriptions.products` is non-empty. If products fail to load, the user sees `emptyState` (`:84`) with **no Restore and no legal links**; the `offlinePlaceholder` (`:306-321`) likewise has neither. A reviewer on a throttled/fresh review device who hits the paywall before products resolve sees a paywall with no Restore button — a frequent, specific 3.1.2 rejection cause.

**Fix:** Move **Restore Purchases** and the **Terms of Use / Privacy Policy** links *outside* the product-loaded branch so they're always present (including loading/empty/offline). The price/trial disclosure can stay tied to a selected plan.

### H2 — The paywall is never proactively shown — conversion is structurally capped ✅
*This directly undercuts your #1 goal.* The only two entry points are passive cards: `SettingsView.swift:185` (pro postcard) and `HistoryView.swift:191` (pro preview card). `App.swift` never presents `PaywallView`; onboarding finishes straight into calibration with no upsell; a good scan / streak milestone only fires the **review** prompt (`AcknowledgmentView.swift:256-261`), never the paywall. A new user can onboard, calibrate, scan, build a streak, and **never once see the trial offer**.

**Fix:** Add 1–2 well-timed, dismissible soft paywalls: (a) a celebratory moment after the first "good" scan or first streak milestone — "keep your whole streak, try Posture+ free for 7 days"; (b) one full-screen paywall at the end of onboarding/calibration framed around AirPods background coaching. Keep the free tier usable, but ensure every new user sees the offer at least once. The existing positive-moment infrastructure (`ReviewPromptTracker`) is the ideal trigger to reuse.

### H3 — Review notes require AirPods but give the reviewer no testable path; `audio` background mode unjustified-on-its-face (2.5.4) ✅ / ⚠️
`review_information/notes.txt:6` tells the reviewer to test "on a device with compatible AirPods connected" and walks an AirPods-only flow — with **no instructions for a reviewer who lacks AirPods**, and (pre-B1) no in-binary path for them either. Separately, `Info.plist:29-32` declares `UIBackgroundModes: [audio]` and `AirpodsBackgroundMonitor.swift:332-354` plays a near-silent buffer (`mainMixerNode.volume = 0.01`) purely to keep CoreMotion alive in the background — the classic "silent audio to keep a task alive" pattern Apple scrutinizes under 2.5.4. The in-app disclosure for it (`SettingsView.swift:48, :172-177`) is good, but it lives behind the calibration gate the reviewer can't pass.

**Fix:** After B1 lands, update notes to point at the real AirPods-free path ("tap *Continue without AirPods*, then use *check in by hand*…"). Pre-emptively justify the background-audio mode in notes (only iOS mechanism for continuous AirPods head-motion; off by default; Pro-gated; orange-dot disclosed; no audio recorded). Be prepared for pushback regardless — it remains a real risk.

### H4 — Motion-permission denial is silently indistinguishable from "no AirPods" ✅
There is **no CoreMotion authorization handling anywhere** (grep for `authorizationStatus`/`.denied`/`.restricted` finds only `notificationsDenied`). `HeadphoneMotionService.start()` just calls `beginMotionUpdates()` and never inspects auth. A user who has AirPods but taps "Don't Allow" on the motion prompt gets the misleading "We need compatible AirPods" wall (`CalibrationView.swift:231`) with no hint the real cause is the permission, and no recovery.

**Fix:** Check `CMHeadphoneMotionManager.authorizationStatus()`; if `.denied`/`.restricted`, show a distinct "Motion access is off" state with a button to `UIApplication.openSettingsURLString` — the exact pattern already used for notifications at `SettingsView.swift:210-217`.

### H5 — Keywords waste a slot on "camera" and omit high-intent terms ✅
`keywords.txt` (90/100 chars) spends a slot on the inaccurate "camera" while high-volume queries — *posture corrector, slouch, hunchback, text neck, neck pain* — are absent. Lower discovery = fewer installs = fewer trial starts.

**Fix:** Drop `camera`. Don't repeat name/subtitle words (posture/neck/desk/slouch are already covered there). Comma-separate **with no spaces**. Candidate: `back,spine,hunchback,text neck,neck pain,corrector,reminder,ergonomic,airpods,sit,wfh,office` (verify ≤100 after upload; trim any subtitle overlap).

## 🟡 MEDIUM

- **M1 — Trial eligibility defaults to `true`** ⚠️ (`SubscriptionService.swift:195-198`: `introEligibility[id] ?? true`). Before eligibility resolves, an ineligible user (e.g. resubscribing) can see "Try Posture+ 7-day free trial", the "Start Free Trial" CTA (`PaywallView.swift:329`), and the "free trial, then $X" disclosure — then get charged immediately with no trial. Trust hit + refund requests + 3.1.2 accuracy complaint. **Fix:** default unknown → `false`, or show a neutral "Continue/Start Posture+" until eligibility confirms.

- **M2 — Manual / no-AirPods check-in can never record a posture quality** ✅. Both manual paths pass `quality: nil` (`AcknowledgmentView.swift:86, :119`); there's no UI to self-report good/borderline/bad. So `alignmentScore` (`TodayView.swift:48`) shows "—" forever and History bars never color in for an AirPods-less user. The app is honest about it ("Alignment score needs a quick scan"), and the streak still advances — but the central value prop is invisible to them. **Fix:** replace the single "just checking in" button with three taps (aligned / drifting / slouched) that record a real `PostureQuality`. This also makes the app fully demonstrable without AirPods (mitigates B1/2.1).

- **M3 — "Full posture history" overclaim** ✅(corrected). `description.txt:16` lists "Full posture history and trend view" under FREE, but the paywall says "Free shows your last 7 days" (`PaywallView.swift:189`). *Note: the audit's original claim that History depth is code-gated is wrong — both tiers get the same 7-day strip + 14-day query; the actual Pro gate is the 24h passive timeline, not history depth.* So this is a **copy contradiction**, not a functional wall. **Fix:** align the description copy with the paywall framing.

- **M4 — AirPods/Watch framed as "supported" when required** ⚠️ (`description.txt:3`, `promotional_text.txt:1`). A scored scan needs AirPods; without them you get hand-logging only. Stating the requirement honestly up front also pre-empts the 2.1 reviewer concern.

- **M5 — 30s silent wait in calibration with no feedback** ⚠️ (`CalibrationView.swift:41`, waiting step `:68-102` has no timer/progress). Reads as frozen. **Fix:** surface a "Don't have AirPods handy? Continue without them" affordance after ~5–8s (ties into B1) and a subtle "searching…" indicator.

- **M6 — AirPods scan "waiting" has no timeout** ⚠️ (`AirpodsScanView.swift:183-192` only routes to `.noConnection` when `!isAvailable`). If available-but-not-connected, it can pulse on "pop your AirPods in" indefinitely. **Fix:** mirror CalibrationView's deadline (~10–15s) → auto-advance to the noConnection view (which has the prominent "check in by hand").

- **M7 — Losing AirPods mid-scan discards the check-in** ⚠️ (`AirpodsScanView.swift:212-216`). Full 3s hold, buds hiccup at second 3 → bounced to a "can't hear your AirPods" wall, samples discarded, nothing recorded. **Fix:** score from samples already gathered, or auto-record a manual streak-credit and show the done state.

- **M8 — `release_notes.txt` is empty** ⚠️. Fine for a first submission, required for updates. Add an honest pivot note (e.g. "Posture now reads your alignment through your AirPods' motion sensor — no camera needed…"), localized.

- **M9 — Paywall trust strip "On-device / Private by default" sits above the silent-audio background feature** ⚠️ (`PaywallView.swift:152-159` vs the "Quiet AirPods background coaching" benefit `:181-185`, which lights the orange dot). Reads as deceptive to a privacy-conscious reviewer/user. **Fix:** add a one-line honest disclosure on the paywall, or soften the absolute privacy claim.

- **M10 — Lifetime plan ($79.99) has no value framing or anchor** ⚠️ (`Posture.storekit`, `PaywallView.swift:517-519`). Neither converts nor anchors yearly. **Fix:** add break-even framing ("best after ~2.5 years · never renews") or use it deliberately as the top anchor; consider whether 3 tiers + lifetime is too many choices (A/B a 2-tier default with lifetime behind "see all plans").

## The conversion story (your explicit goal)

Even after approval, the trial → paid funnel is throttled at two points:
1. **Top of funnel:** a large slice of installs never get past calibration (B1), so they never reach *any* monetizable surface.
2. **Mid funnel:** of those who do, **most never see the paywall** (H2) — it only appears if they wander into Settings or History and tap a soft card.

Fixing B1 + H2 + M2 (let AirPods-less users in, show the offer at a peak moment, and give everyone a visible posture readout) is what turns this from "ships but doesn't convert" into "ships and converts." Do those alongside the compliance blockers, not after.
