# Posture UX Audit & Suggested Improvements

This document outlines recommended improvements to the **Posture** user experience (UX) based on code analysis, user feedback (via review logs), and the MVP release audit. Every recommendation is centered around **how a user operates and experiences the app**, identifying friction points in their journey and proposing concrete solutions.

---

## 1. Onboarding & First-Touch Experience

### A. The AirPods Ownership Assumption
* **User Experience & Friction**: 
  Currently, [OnboardingView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/OnboardingView.swift) forces the user through a "ready your AirPods" step. Tapping "continue" hardcodes `settings.hasAirpods = true` behind the scenes. 
  If a user **does not own AirPods**, they are immediately funneled into [CalibrationView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/CalibrationView.swift)'s AirPods calibration page. Since no AirPods are connected, the "Calibrate" button is disabled and the user is **completely locked out** from using the app unless they force close it.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  Modify the onboarding screen to ask: *"Do you use AirPods?"*
  - Provide two clear buttons: 
    - **"Yes, calibrate with AirPods"** (sets `settings.hasAirpods = true` and launches AirPods calibration).
    - **"No, use iPhone camera instead"** (sets `settings.hasAirpods = false` and launches front-camera calibration).
  - This removes the blocker for non-AirPods users and routes them cleanly to the camera-only experience.

### B. Clarifying Baseline Calibration Instructions
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  When users calibrate, they are instructed to "Hold still" or "Now slouch". 
  - **"Hold still"** lacks context; the user does not know *why* they are holding still or how long it will take.
  - **"Now slouch"** (as noted in review notes) is confusing and feels unnecessary. While the current codebase has removed the interactive slouching step and hardcodes a slouch threshold (`slouchPitchDelta: .pi / 24`), the instructions and references in comments/docs still suggest it.
  - In camera mode, the "Capture" button is disabled when the face is out of frame, but there is no explanation explaining why it is disabled.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - Update the description text in [CalibrationView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/CalibrationView.swift) to add context: *“Sit tall and hold still to set your baseline posture.”*
  - Ensure the countdown screen has a clear 5-second indicator.
  - If the camera "Capture" button is disabled due to no face detection, display a gentle helper message: *“Align your face inside the guide to begin.”*

## 2. The Daily Nudge & Scan Loop

### A. Add an Escape Route to Check-ins
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  Tapping a reminder or clicking "Check in now" on [TodayView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/TodayView.swift) opens [AcknowledgmentView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/AcknowledgmentView.swift) as a `fullScreenCover`. 
  Currently, the close/dismiss button is hidden unless the user arrived via a scheduled notification tap with a non-zero index (`notificationIndex != nil && idx > 0`). If a user opens the sheet manually or taps the first reminder, they are trapped on the screen and forced to either complete a scan or select manual log.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - Show the close (`xmark`) button **unconditionally** in the top-right corner of the choice screen in `AcknowledgmentView`. 
  - Tapping close should dismiss the sheet without saving a record, respecting user control.

### B. Fallback from AirPods Scan to Camera Scan
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  If a user has calibrated with AirPods, the check-in defaults to [AirpodsScanView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/Components/AirpodsScanView.swift). If their AirPods are not connected, the screen displays a waiting status. The only option presented is to "check in by hand" (manual log with no measurement).
  Although the parent view supports switching to the camera via an `onUseCamera` handler, `AirpodsScanView` never exposes this button to the user.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - Add a **"Use Camera scan instead"** button in `AirpodsScanView` when AirPods are not connected.
  - Tapping it should fire the `onUseCamera` callback to switch the user directly to the camera-based `QuickScanView`, preventing them from having to settle for a manual log.

### C. Soften "Not Tracking" Feedback
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  During camera scans in [QuickScanView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/Components/QuickScanView.swift), if a face is briefly lost, the status pill flashes a red dot with a jarring warning. Users find this error-focused feedback scolding and frustrating.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - Tone down the visual urgency. Instead of an alarming red dot, use a warm neutral color (such as `Theme.sand`) to indicate search status.
  - Change the label from *"find your face"* to a supportive instruction: *"Bring your face back into the frame."*
  - Allow the 3-second scan to pause and resume gracefully rather than failing immediately if a face is lost for a fraction of a second.

## 3. Background Monitoring & Settings Clarity

### A. Address iOS vs. Watch Always-On Toggle Sync
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  In [SettingsView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/SettingsView.swift), there is an "Always-on Watch monitoring" toggle. Tapping it writes to iOS local settings, but has **no effect** on the Apple Watch unless the user manually launches the Watch app. This leaves users believing the background feature is broken.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - When the user toggles "Always-on Watch monitoring" on iOS, show an informational popup: *“Make sure to open Posture on your Apple Watch to sync and start background tracking.”*
  - Synchronize settings immediately via `WCSession` so that the watch app starts or stops background sessions reactively when next opened.

### B. Transparency Regarding the AirPods Background Audio Indicator
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  To monitor posture passively with AirPods, [AirpodsBackgroundMonitor.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Shared/Services/AirpodsBackgroundMonitor.swift) plays silent audio in the background to prevent iOS from suspending the motion data stream. This causes an orange mic/audio indicator to appear in the Dynamic Island / status bar. Users find this suspicious and worry the app is recording them.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - In Settings, expand the description below the toggle: *“Posture plays a silent tone to keep the AirPods sensor active while your phone is locked. No audio is recorded.”*
  - Alternatively, deprecate AirPods background monitoring in favor of Apple Watch background tracking (which uses standard HealthKit `HKWorkoutSession` APIs and doesn't require audio hacks).

## 4. Gamification vs. Mindful Coaching

### A. De-emphasize Duolingo-style Streaks on Onboarding
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  Gamifying posture correction with aggressive streak counters on first launch can feel high-pressure and out of place for a calm wellness app. Review notes show the user prefers not to have Duolingo-style streaks on the initial customer screens.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - Remove all streak references and the `StreakFlame` component from onboarding and initial screens.
  - Move the active streak counter to a quiet, elegant numeric representation in the navigation bar toolbar (e.g., *“3 days”* in [Theme.displaySerif](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Shared/Utilities/Theme.swift#L89) italic) rather than a bright orange flame button.

### B. Graceful Loading States for the Paywall
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  On a slow internet connection, tapping the upgrade button shows a generic placeholder paywall screen. If RevenueCat finishes loading packages seconds later, the layout shifts abruptly.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - In [PaywallView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/PaywallView.swift), display a skeleton load state or a beautiful "Loading plans..." message before presenting the packages, ensuring a smooth visual transition.

## 5. Trust, Permissions, & Baseline Drift

### A. Denied Camera Permission Behavior
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  In [QuickScanView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/Components/QuickScanView.swift), if camera permission is denied, the code falls back to returning a deviation of `0`, which logs the check-in as a `.good` (aligned) scan. This breaks app integrity by rewarding the user for a scan that never happened.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - When camera permission is denied, route the user to a dedicated "Camera Access Required" sub-page with a link to open Settings.
  - Do not record a mock "good scan". Instead, encourage them to log a manual check-in: *"We couldn't scan your posture. Log this check-in manually?"*

### B. Graceful Drift & Recalibration Prompts
* **User Experience & Friction**:
  Because AirPods sit slightly differently in the ear each time they are put on, and because general postures shift, calibration baselines naturally drift over weeks.
* **Suggested Actionable Improvement**:
  - The current soft banner in [TodayView.swift](file:///Users/jackwallner/posture/Posture/Views/TodayView.swift) triggers after 30 days. Enhance this experience by keeping it encouraging and simple: *“Things drift over time. Let's take 5 seconds to reset your baseline.”* and guiding them directly to a quick one-step calibration process.
